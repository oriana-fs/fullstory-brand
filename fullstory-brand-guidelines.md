| | |

| **Vision** | To be the digital sight that empowers businesses to perfect digital experiences in real-time. |

## 02 — Tone of Voice

Our brand voice is built from four pillars. Each shapes the words we choose, the stories we tell, and the impression we leave.

### Relatable
We speak like a knowledgeable colleague — friendly, genuine, and human. Never robotic or jargon-heavy.

| It is | It is not |
|-------|-----------|
| Friendly · Genuine · Welcoming · Human-centric | Unpolished · Slangy · Overtly casual |

> *"We built this for the teams who never get to see the full picture — until now."*

### Adept
We empower and inspire through confident, impactful language. We never discourage or patronize.

| It is | It is not |
|-------|-----------|
| Empowering · Inspiring · Confident · Impactful | Negative · Discouraging · Patronizing |

> *"Every session holds an answer. FullStory helps you find it."*

### Uplifting
We draw on deep expertise to share timely, authoritative insight — without ego or speculation.

| It is | It is not |
|-------|-----------|
| Educated · Timely · Authoritative | Egotistical · Cocky · Biased · Speculative |

> *"The data doesn't lie — and neither do we. Here's what enterprise teams are actually doing."*

### Honest
We say what we mean — with kindness and precision. No spin, no vague promises, no misleading framing.

| It is | It is not |
|-------|-----------|
| Realistic · Kind · Informative | Blunt · Cold · Misleading |

> *"Improvement takes time. We're here for the whole journey — not just the highlight reel."*

### Clear Space & Minimum Size

The minimum clear space on all sides of the logo equals the cap-height of the "f" in FullStory (referred to as *x*). No other graphic, image, or text elements may fall within this area.

> **Exception:** Clear space may be reduced when the FullStory logo appears with partner, customer, or event logos, to reinforce the idea of partnership and collaboration.

| Format | Full Logo | Monogram |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Print  | 1 in      | 0.35 in  |
| Screen | 108 px    | 28 px    |

Do not use the monogram when there is sufficient space to use the primary logo.

### Logo Relationships

When the FullStory logo appears alongside a partner or customer logo:

| Relationship | Separator | Clearspace |
|---|---|---|
| Partner | `+` | ½x |
| Customer | `×` | ½x |
| Website (descriptive text) | Vertical bar `\|` | Standard |
| Event / Sponsorship | None | ½x |

- `+` reinforces the concept of building and adding to a technology stack
- `×` reinforces the concept of amplification — how FullStory amplifies the customer's ability to perfect their digital experience
- In almost all contexts, partner, customer, and event logos should appear in **all black or all white**

**Sub-brand destinations:**

| Token | Destination |
|-------|-------------|
| `fsdev` | dev.fullstory.com |
| `fshelp` | help.fullstory.com |
| `fslearn` | learn.fullstory.com |
| `fseventhub` | Event hub |

### Placement

The FullStory logo may appear in any corner of the given format (top-left, bottom-left, top-right, bottom-right).

- Logo in a corner → **left-align** typography
- Logo centered → **center-align** typography

### Incorrect Use

1. Do not stretch or distort the logo
2. Do not use the previous FullStory heart monogram
3. Do not modify the characters of the wordmark
4. Do not add blur effects or drop shadows
5. Do not add graphics to the logo or monogram
6. Do not fill the logo with colors or gradients
7. Do not use old or outdated logos
8. Do not use the logo as a watermark
9. Do not fill the logo with patterns

## 05 — Typography

Typography is a foundational component of our identity and plays an important role in creating a consistent look across every communication — from internal emails to marketing materials to building signage.

### Core Typeface: Neulis

The **Neulis** type family features three distinct sub-families, each serving a specific role:

| Sub-family | Role | Primary use |
|------------|------|-------------|
| **Neulis Cursive** | Display | H1 headlines and accent text — highest hierarchy only |
| **Neulis Neue** | Secondary display | H2–H5 headlines and accent text |
| **Neulis Sans** | Body | Paragraphs, UI text, and captions |

All three sub-families include 10 weights (Hairline → Black) with italic variants — 60 files total.

**Font access:** [Adobe Fonts (Typekit)](https://use.typekit.net/jbp5lty.css) · [Download zip](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/oriana-fs/fullstory-brand/main/Neulis%20Font%20Family.zip)

**Fallback:** When Neulis is unavailable, use **Poppins** (Google Fonts) — required for Google Slides and Documents.

### Type Scale

| Style | Typeface | Desktop | Mobile |
|-------|----------|---------|--------|
| **H1 Display** | Neulis Cursive Regular | 70pt / 4.375rem | 48pt / 3rem |
| **H1 Bold** | Neulis Neue Bold | 62pt / 3.875rem | 48pt / 3rem |
| **H2** | Neulis Neue Bold | 48pt / 3rem | 40pt / 2.5rem |
| **H3** | Neulis Neue Bold | 36pt / 2.25rem | 32pt / 2rem |
| **H4** | Neulis Sans Bold | 28pt / 1.75rem | 24pt / 1.5rem |
| **H5 Eyebrow** | Neulis Sans Bold | 24pt / 1.5rem | 20pt / 1.25rem |
| **Body** | Neulis Sans Regular | 20pt / 1rem | 16pt / 1rem |
| **CTA** | Neulis Sans Regular | 20pt / 1rem | 16pt / 1rem |

> CTAs are always inline text links in **Lilac Deep (`#9E36F1`)** — always include a right arrow glyph `→` after the text.

### Neulis Cursive Typesetting Guide

Neulis Cursive requires manual customization after setting. Always review type carefully — additional kerning adjustments may still be needed.

**1. Kerning**
Reduce letterspacing by **2–4%** (weight-dependent) for a bolder, tighter look. Target: visually balanced spacing — not so tight letterforms collide, not so loose the headline reads as disconnected.

**2. Replace lowercase "r"**
The cursive "r" resembles the Pi (π) symbol. Replace all lowercase "r" in Neulis Cursive with **Neulis Neue**.

**3. Replace double "ll"**
Double "ll" can appear too busy. Replace "ll" characters with **Neulis Neue**.
> Example: "excellent" — the "ll" should be set in Neulis Neue

**4. Replace select "s"**
The cursive "s" can create visual noise at high frequency. Replace double "ss" or distracting single "s" instances with **Neulis Neue**.

### General Typesetting Rules

| Rule | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| **Kerning** | Space between characters should be visually balanced — review all set type carefully |
| **Leading** | Incorrect leading (too large or too small) reduces legibility regardless of typeface quality |
| **Ragging** | Lines should gently alternate long/short — avoid too jagged or too straight |
| **Widows & Orphans** | Minimize single words or short isolated lines through manual breaks or copy adjustments |

## 06 — Color

Our color palette consists of five brilliant hues — vibrant yet rich — that visually identify FullStory in our industry.

**Application rules:**
- Do not use more than two color families on a single page (unless creating data visualizations)
- Ensure proper contrast when pairing core colors
- **Emerald is the designated color for links, buttons, and CTAs**

## 07 — Imagery

### Iconography

Simple images rendered in a minimal style. Icons should not be used alone as illustrations.

**Style rules:**
- Clean, blocky shapes — not always perfect
- Overlaid linework for detail and motion
- Two colors per icon
- Visible layering of components

[Download icon zip](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/oriana-fs/fullstory-brand/main/icons.zip)

### Illustration

Combines line art, solids, and gradients to depict abstract interactions. Housed within our signature blob shapes, illustrations create a proprietary visual language unique to the FullStory brand.

[View illustration library in Figma](https://www.figma.com/design/9u5JHL9ssjbRW8fmwqY0gU/2.0-Fullstory-Branding?node-id=1230-383)

### Photography

Used to personify the user's experience and show the real-world impact of FullStory. Photography should feel authentic, warm, and purposeful — never staged or stock-generic.

[View photography library in Figma](https://www.figma.com/design/9u5JHL9ssjbRW8fmwqY0gU/2.0-Fullstory-Branding?node-id=975-468)

### Product Visuals

Screenshots and product UI captures used in marketing. Always use the most current UI and pair with brand-color backgrounds or illustration elements for context.

[View product visuals in Figma](https://www.figma.com/design/9u5JHL9ssjbRW8fmwqY0gU/2.0-Fullstory-Branding)
