01 — Identity

Who we are

We are the behavioral data platform for enterprises that need to eliminate blind spots that kill conversion and waste operational resources.

Mission
To optimize every digital interaction.
Vision
To be the digital sight that empowers businesses to perfect digital experiences in real-time.

Our values

Empathy
We lead with curiosity and genuine care — for our customers, our teammates, and the humans behind every digital interaction.
Clarity
We cut through noise with precise, honest communication. We make complex things understandable without oversimplifying what matters.
Trust
We earn trust through consistency, transparency, and doing what we say. Our brand, our data, and our people are reliable by design.

02 — Tone of Voice

How we speak

Our brand voice is built from four pillars. Each shapes the words we choose, the stories we tell, and the impression we leave.

Relatable
We speak like a knowledgeable colleague — friendly, genuine, and human. Never robotic or jargon-heavy.
It is
  • Friendly
  • Genuine
  • Welcoming
  • Human-centric
It is not
  • Unpolished
  • Slangy
  • Overtly casual
Example
"We built this for the teams who never get to see the full picture — until now."
Adept
We empower and inspire through confident, impactful language. We never discourage or patronize.
It is
  • Empowering
  • Inspiring
  • Confident
  • Impactful
It is not
  • Negative
  • Discouraging
  • Patronizing
Example
"Every session holds an answer. FullStory helps you find it."
Uplifting
We draw on deep expertise to share timely, authoritative insight — without ego or speculation.
It is
  • Educated
  • Timely
  • Authoritative
It is not
  • Egotistical
  • Cocky
  • Biased
  • Speculative
Example
"The data doesn't lie — and neither do we. Here's what enterprise teams are actually doing."
Honest
We say what we mean — with kindness and precision. No spin, no vague promises, no misleading framing.
It is
  • Realistic
  • Kind
  • Informative
It is not
  • Blunt
  • Cold
  • Misleading
Example
"Improvement takes time. We're here for the whole journey — not just the highlight reel."

05 — Typography

Core typography

Display — H1 headlines only

Neulis Cursive typesetting

Cursive should only be set in headlines. Due to its character-substitution system, always check glyph rendering. Avoid setting Cursive below 32px.

  • Manual kerning required on display sizes
  • Never use automated tracking on Cursive
  • Limit to one Cursive headline per layout

General typesetting rules

Maintain visual rhythm through consistent use of the type scale. Do not introduce additional font sizes outside the hierarchy.

  • Left-align body text — centered only for short captions
  • Max line length: 70–80 characters for body text
  • Never set body text below 13px
  • Fallback: Poppins for Google Workspace
